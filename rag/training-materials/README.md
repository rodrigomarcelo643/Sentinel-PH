# Training Materials

## Overview

Educational content for community sentinels on how to observe, recognize, and report health patterns in their communities.

## Training Modules

### Module 1: Introduction to Community Surveillance
- What is a community sentinel?
- Why your observations matter
- How SentinelPH works
- Your role in outbreak prevention

### Module 2: What to Observe
- Medication purchase patterns
- Illness mentions in conversations
- Absence patterns (school, work, gatherings)
- Environmental health concerns
- Behavioral changes in community

### Module 3: How to Report
- Using the mobile app
- Describing observations clearly
- Location tagging
- Photo documentation
- When to report

### Module 4: Trust Score System
- How trust scores work
- Building your reliability
- Verification process
- Rewards and recognition

### Module 5: Spam Prevention
- What NOT to report
- Avoiding false alarms
- Respecting privacy
- Consequences of misuse

## Observation Guidelines

### Good Observations ✅

**Medication Purchases**:
- "5 customers bought Biogesic today, more than usual"
- "Running low on ORS, many people asking for it"
- "Several families buying cough medicine this week"

**Illness Mentions**:
- "3 neighbors mentioned their kids have fever"
- "Tricycle passengers talking about diarrhea"
- "Church members absent due to flu-like symptoms"

**Absence Patterns**:
- "10 children absent from Sunday school, parents say fever"
- "Market vendor closed, family sick"
- "Fewer people at community center, many staying home"

**Environmental**:
- "Standing water in vacant lot after rain"
- "Increased mosquitoes near creek"
- "Water supply looks cloudy today"

### Poor Observations ❌

- "Someone is sick" (too vague)
- "I think there's an outbreak" (speculation)
- "My neighbor has COVID" (diagnosis, privacy violation)
- "Everyone is buying medicine" (exaggeration)
- Repeated identical reports (spam)

- A) Your neighbor has dengue ❌
- B) Many people buying paracetamol ✅
- C) You think there's an outbreak ❌

**Question 2**: How often can you submit observations?

## Certification

### Trial Period (First 5 Observations)
- All observations reviewed by BHW
- Feedback provided
- Corrections made
- Learning opportunity

### Active Status
- Trust score ≥ 50
- Completed training
- Passed comprehension check
- 5 observations verified

### Elite Status
- Trust score ≥ 85
- 30+ verified observations
- 6+ months active
- Special recognition
